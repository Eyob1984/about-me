I started my professional career in the Air Force as a Visual Imagery & Intrusion Detction Systems technician, or VIIDS for short. Working along side the video production team at Lackland AFB, I became interested in video editing. After leaving the Air Force, I pursued a Bachelor of Arts in Electronic Media at the Universityof Texas at El Paso.

I started working at the local ABC station, KVIA, editing videos for air during the Good Morning El Paso show. After which, I moved to Seattle & worked at KING for about a year. Then, the recession of 2009, upended everything.

I went back to my electronic technician background and began working at a remanufacturing company for automotice electronics, Instrument Sales & Service. It was there I got my first taste of coding by teaching myself enough C#to create a small application for automatically calibrating digital ratio adapter buffers (DRABs) for old pick-up trucks.

Through a referral from a co-worker at Instrument Sales & Service, I was able to land a job as a validation engineer at Zonar Systems. During my time there I was able to learn a great deal from working with firmware developers & my fellow validation engineers. I was able to teach myself a fair amount of SQL in order to parse and auto-generate daily reports in Google Studio.

Now, I am looking to take the experience I've gained over the past 2 decades and combine it with new skills learned at Code Fellows to accelerate my career forward.
